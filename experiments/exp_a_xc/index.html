Social Cognitive Neuroscience Laboratory

Because this HIT uses videos, a fast internet connection is required!

Note: You will need to be able to play audio in order to successfully complete this HIT.

In this experiment, we are interested in how well you can read other people's reactions!

This HIT consists of 88 trials in which you will watch short (~14 sec) videos and decide what recently happened to the person from a small set of options. The videos are of people's natural and spontaneous reactions (not of staged actors).

BONUS: If you are especially good at reading people's reactions (>85% correct) you will receive a $4 bonus.


Disclaimer: This HIT is part of a scientific research project. Your decision to complete this HIT is voluntary. There is no way for us to identify you. The only information we will have, in addition to your responses, is the time at which you completed the survey. The results of the research may be presented at scientific meetings or published in scientific journals. Clicking on the 'Next' button on the bottom of this page indicates that you are at least 18 years of age, and agree to complete this HIT voluntarily.

Instructions 1 of 3:

In this experiment, you will decide what you think happened to people based on short videos.


All of the people you will consider were one of the two contestants in the final round of a gameshow. These are real people winning (or not winning) real money.

Leading up to this round, these final two contestants interacted in games involving strategic deception and honesty to add money to the total jackpot, and have managed to avoid being eliminated in the earlier rounds.

In this round, all of the money that the two contestants have accumulated is at stake. These jackpots can range from $2.00 to $100,000.00.

Each contestant is given a choice: whether to 'Split' or 'Steal'.


If both people choose to 'Split', they each win half of the total jackpot.

If one player chooses to 'Steal' while the other player chooses to 'Split', the player who Stole wins the entire jackpot while the player who Split wins nothing.

If both players choose to 'Steal', then they both leave having won nothing.

Next you'll hear the rules of the game described and watch an example of two contestants negotiating before revealing their 'Split' or 'Steal' decisions.

This is the only time that audio will be required for this HIT.
